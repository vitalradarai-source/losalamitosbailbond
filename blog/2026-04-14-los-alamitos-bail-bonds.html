Where Los Alamitos Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Los Alamitos is transported to the Los Alamitos Police Department Detention Facility at 3201 Katella Avenue, Los Alamitos, CA 90720 (phone: 562-431-2255). The Los Alamitos Police Department operates its own Type I detention facility inside the police headquarters on Katella Avenue. It handles the initial booking for most arrests made inside Los Alamitos city limits before any transfer into the county system.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Angels Bail Bonds posts bonds at every one of these facilities.

Los Alamitos in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Los Alamitos is one of the few cities in the West Orange County corridor that still operates its own jail. The Los Alamitos Police Department Jail at 3201 Katella Avenue handles initial booking for arrests inside city limits. This is relevant because a bond can often be posted at LAPD before any transfer happens, meaning the fastest release is possible when families call immediately.

The city is built around the Los Alamitos Race Course and the Joint Forces Training Base (JFTB), both of which have their own jurisdictional quirks. Arrests that start on base are handled by military police and do not go through LAPD at all — different rules, different bail process, and a bondsman cannot post there. Civilian arrests off the 405 freeway, the 605, or along Katella Avenue and Los Alamitos Boulevard all route through the LAPD city jail.

For cases that escalate beyond the city jail's capacity or for felonies, defendants transfer to the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange — a 25-minute drive depending on traffic on the 22 freeway. The LAPD jail itself has limited capacity, so weekend transfers happen quickly. The time-sensitive play is posting a bond at the LAPD jail before that transfer window closes, because post-transfer release can add 4–8 hours.

Typical Late-Night Arrest Scenarios in Los Alamitos

A DUI arrest on the 605 freeway near the Katella exit — LAPD responds, books at the city jail on Katella Avenue. Same-night release is common when a bondsman reaches the jail before the 2 AM shift change.

A public intoxication or bar-fight call from the Los Alamitos Boulevard restaurant strip — PC 647(f) or PC 242 are the typical charges. LAPD jail can process these fast, but the defendant may be held until sober before any release paperwork moves.

A domestic violence call from one of the residential neighborhoods between Katella and Cerritos Avenue — PC 273.5 books at $50,000 at the city jail, then transfers to Theo Lacy if bail is not posted within a few hours.

How a Los Alamitos Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — los alamitos bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at LAPD (Los Alamitos) Jail and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Los Alamitos arrest actually get processed?

Los Alamitos has its own city jail — the LAPD Jail at 3201 Katella Avenue. Bonds can be posted here directly for misdemeanor charges, with release often within 1–2 hours. Felonies and overflow transfer to Theo Lacy Facility in Orange.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Los Alamitos?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Los Alamitos bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Los Alamitos DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
