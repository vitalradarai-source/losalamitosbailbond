This guide explains the difference under California law and how it shapes your bail decisions.

The Legal Definition

Under California Penal Code §17:

  • Misdemeanor: Punishable by up to 1 year in county jail and/or a fine up to $1,000-$2,000
  • Felony: Punishable by 16 months, 2 years, or 3 years in state prison; capital crimes carry life or death
  • Infraction: Punishable only by fine — minor traffic or code violations, no jail, no bail

Why Wobblers Matter

A "wobbler" is a charge the prosecutor can file as either misdemeanor or felony based on:

  • Defendant's prior record
  • Severity of injury or property damage
  • Whether a weapon was involved
  • Vulnerability of the victim (minor, elderly, disabled)
  • Aggravating circumstances at the scene

For wobblers, bail is initially set at the felony level pending the prosecutor's filing decision (within 48-72 hours of arrest). Once formally filed:

  • Filed as misdemeanor: Bail typically drops, conditions relax
  • Filed as felony: Higher bail stands; case proceeds to felony arraignment at Central Justice Center

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between misdemeanor and felony?
CA PC §17 — misdemeanor: up to 1 year county jail; felony: 16 months+ state prison. Misdemeanors carry lower bail, faster release, less long-term impact on rights.
How much higher is felony bail in OC?
Misdemeanor: $500-$25,000. Felony: $25,000-$1M+. The 10% premium scales with the bail amount under CA Ins Code §1800.4.
Can felony bail be denied?
Per CA Constitution Art I §12, bail is generally available except for capital crimes, certain violent felonies with substantial likelihood of harm, and serious sexual assault.
How does processing differ?
Misdemeanor: schedule-based, 6-12 hr custody. Felony: requires judge's hearing within 48 hr, transferred to Theo Lacy or Central Men's Jail, 24-72 hr custody.
What's a wobbler?
A charge the prosecutor can file as either misdemeanor or felony based on circumstances. Bail initially set at felony level, drops if filed as misdemeanor.
