First-Time DUI Bail in Los Alamitos (VC § 23152 Guide)

What a first DUI booking actually costs, the 10-day DMV trap, and how to get released same-night.

The Two DUI Charges That Drive Bail

Vehicle Code § 23152 — Driving Under the Influence (no injury). The standard misdemeanor first-offense DUI. It applies whether the impairment is alcohol (VC § 23152(a)/(b)) or drugs (VC § 23152(f)). For a first offense without aggravating factors, the OC 2026 schedule generally sets bail at $2,500 to $5,000 for misdemeanor cases.

Vehicle Code § 23153 — DUI Causing Injury. The felony version. If the impaired driving caused injury to anyone other than the driver, the case is filed under VC § 23153 and bail starts at $100,000 on the OC schedule. Multiple injuries or great bodily injury allegations under PC § 12022.7 can push it higher.

What Pushes a First DUI Bail Higher

Even on a "first-time" DUI, several factors increase the bail amount before any judicial review:

  • BAC ≥ 0.15 — VC § 23578 enhancement, often raises bail
  • Refusal of chemical test — VC § 23612 implied-consent enhancement; also a 1-year DMV suspension
  • Child passenger under 14 — VC § 23572 enhancement; mandatory jail time on conviction
  • Speed ≥ 30 mph over the limit — VC § 23582; reckless-driving enhancement
  • Accident involvement — even a minor fender-bender can re-class to VC § 23153
  • Open container — VC § 23222(a); separate but bookable

Where Los Alamitos DUI Cases Are Booked

Los Alamitos has its own police department. Bookings happen at the Los Alamitos Police Department Jail at 3561 Howard Avenue (phone: 562-431-2233). The facility is small — 24-hour holding cells with a bond clerk on duty. Most first-time misdemeanor DUI defendants release on bond directly from LAPD jail, often within 2–4 hours of the bond posting.

Felony DUI defendants (VC § 23153 or those with priors making the case a wobbler under PC § 17(b)) transfer to OC Central Men's Jail or OC Central Women's Jail at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana. Transfer adds 4–8 hours to processing.

The DMV 10-Day Trap

This is the single most missed deadline in California DUI defense. When an officer makes a DUI arrest and the chemical test result is over 0.08 (or refused), the officer issues a pink "Order of Suspension and Temporary License" — DMV form DS-367. That paperwork starts a clock.

Under California Vehicle Code § 13558, the driver has 10 calendar days from the arrest date to request an Administrative Per Se (APS) hearing. The hearing can stay the automatic license suspension while the case proceeds. If the 10 days expire without a request, the suspension takes effect on day 30 — typically a 4-month suspension for a first-offense alcohol DUI, 1 year for a refusal.

The DMV process is separate from the criminal court process. The court does not request the DMV hearing automatically. The driver (or attorney) must call the DMV Driver Safety Office. For Los Alamitos arrests, that is the OC Driver Safety Office: (714) 558-4400.

What Happens in the First 30 Days

  1. Day 1–2: Posting bail (or release on own recognizance for many first offenses), receive arraignment paperwork
  2. Day 1–10: Call DMV at (714) 558-4400 to request the APS hearing under VC § 13558. Do not skip this.
  3. Day 1–14: Hire a criminal defense attorney. Many OC firms offer free first-DUI consultations.
  4. Day 14–28: Arraignment — first court date. Plea is usually "not guilty" while the defense investigates.
  5. Day 30: If no DMV hearing was requested, suspension takes effect. With a hearing pending, the temporary license continues until the DMV ruling.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is bail for a first-time DUI in Los Alamitos?

OC 2026 schedule: $2,500 to $5,000 for VC § 23152 misdemeanor first offense. Higher with BAC ≥ 0.15, refusal, child passenger, or speed enhancement. VC § 23153 felony DUI with injury starts at $100,000.

Will I lose my license?

The DMV imposes an Administrative Per Se suspension after a DUI arrest. You have 10 calendar days under VC § 13558 to request a DMV hearing — that hearing can stay the suspension. Miss the 10-day window and you lose that opportunity.

Where will the defendant be held?

Los Alamitos PD Jail at 3561 Howard Avenue. Felony DUI cases transfer to OC Central Jail in Santa Ana.
