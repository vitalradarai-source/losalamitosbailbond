When Los Alamitos PD Transfers to OC County Jails

Los Alamitos PD jail is a small facility — for felony cases, overnight holds, or capacity issues, defendants transfer to either:

  • Theo Lacy Facility, 501 N The City Dr, Orange, CA 92868. Felonies, longer holds.
  • Intake Release Center (IRC), 550 N Flower St, Santa Ana, CA 92703. Initial county-level intake.

Transfer adds 8-12 hours to the release window because intake at the receiving facility partially restarts. The bondsman tracks the transfer and files the bond at whichever facility currently holds the defendant. Calling (626) 478-1062 ensures we file at the correct location without wasted paperwork.

Why Calling During Booking Saves Hours

The single biggest factor in shortening the release window is starting bond paperwork in parallel with booking. Here's why it matters:

  • Bond filing is the gate. The jail cannot release the defendant until the bond is on file. If the bondsman arrives 30 minutes after booking ends, the family loses 30 minutes. If the bondsman arrives 5 minutes after booking ends with the indemnity agreement already signed, release processing starts immediately.
  • Family verification takes time too. The cosigner needs to provide ID, employment verification, and discuss the payment plan. Doing this during booking removes it from the critical path.
  • Holding-status confirmation. The bondsman calls the jail during booking to confirm the defendant is still at Los Alamitos and hasn't been queued for transfer. Saves filing the bond at the wrong facility.

What Happens After Release — Arraignment

Most Los Alamitos arrests arraign at the West Justice Center, 8141 13th St, Westminster, CA 92683 — typically within 48 hours of booking, weekends and holidays excluded. Felony cases sometimes route to the Central Justice Center in Santa Ana. At arraignment:

  • The defendant enters a plea (most often "not guilty" with counsel present).
  • The judge can modify bail conditions.
  • Future court dates are scheduled. Missing any triggers bond forfeiture and a bench warrant.
  • For DV cases, the judge may issue a Criminal Protective Order under PC §136.2 — separate from any bail conditions.
